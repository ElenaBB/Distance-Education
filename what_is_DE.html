Distance education and distance learning are definitions, which are often confused. Distance learning is defined as:

- A new form of correspondence study tied with the use of IT and multimedia.
- An acquisition of new skills and knowledge via learning with technologies at a distance.
- Another mode of study, along with conventional full-time and part-time study, on the basis of traditional and innovative methods, which include telecommunications technologies.
- A goal-oriented process of asynchronous or synchronous interaction of learners and tutors irrespective of their physical location.

Similarly, the term distance education is defined in a variety of ways since the concept education is more complex than learning:

- A form of education with the use of state-of-the-art tools and technologies to deliver education materials to the recipient irrespective of his/her location.
- A set of educational services offered to the public in the educational environment on the basis of a remote information exchange.
- An educational process and system in which all or a significant proportion of the teaching is carried out by someone or something removed in space and time from the learner.
- A social and pedagogical system aimed at facilitating life-long learning of an individual in the frame of distance learning.

The fact that the two concepts of distance education and distance learning are viewed as issues of quite a problematic and vague definition, and considering the analysis of journals, scientific papers, educational sources and legal documents it is suggested these two concepts are used interchangeably in the thesis.
